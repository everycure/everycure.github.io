The Repurposing Of All Drugs, Mapping All Paths (ROADMAP) project aims to answer some fundamental questions about the experience of drug repurposing for rare disease nonprofit organizations and their relevant stakeholders and to design a solution to some of the challenges rare disease organizations are facing through the creation of an interactive “ROADMAP” tool.

About the ROADMAP project

Learn about the Repurposing Of All Drugs, Mapping All Paths (ROADMAP) project by the Castleman Disease Collaborative Network. Learn about the project’s goals, data collection methods, data analysis approaches, research findings, and how the ROADMAP tool was created through synthesizing insights.

Use the ROADMAP tool

Directly interact with the ROADMAP tool, designed to help support rare disease nonprofit organizations in their pursuit of drug repurposing projects. This tool is based on insights from hundreds of rare disease stakeholders, formatted into a 5-step easy to follow process, with potential roadblocks highlighted at every stage.

Explore survey data

Explore the ROADMAP survey data through interactive data visualizations. Gain valuable insights into the perspectives of stakeholders, including rare disease organizations, patients and loved ones, researchers, and physicians, on the state of rare disease drug repurposing.
